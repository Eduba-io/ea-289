Frame

Most teams at your stage automate the wrong layer. Our working split is 60/30/10.

Layer 01 60%

Traditional code and documentation. Specs, decision logs, flight-software change notes, advisor call records, regulatory filings.

Layer 02 30%

Rule-based logic. Pricing floors, mission constraints, data-sharing rules between USSF Task Force Futures and commercial insurers.

Layer 03 10%

Where a language model earns its keep. Drafting the audit trail an underwriter will read. Summarizing the Interceptor rendezvous trace into a reviewer sign-off. Translating ORB Simulator output into a USSF decision brief.

Spend the first engagement separating the three. Everything downstream gets cheaper.

Case

We ran this play for VigilOre, a multi-agent compliance platform working frontier mining operations in the DRC. The pre-engagement state was 160-plus hours of compliance work per review cycle. Post-engagement it runs in under five minutes, with every decision traceable to a named layer. Same multi-agent problem, different frontier. Structured as a scoped engagement so the operator and the advisor have skin in the same outcome.

Paper

The methodology behind it is published: Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM). A layered filesystem (L0 identity through L4 working artifacts) that a three-person team can maintain without drift. Repo and paper: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-. Submitted to ACM TiiS.

For governance-sensitive customers on the actuarial side, Eduba also ships the Ethics Engine, a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. Paper: arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742. Repo: github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine.
